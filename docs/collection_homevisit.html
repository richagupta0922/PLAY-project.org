Now that you have set your target participant sample and completed all the steps listed in the recruitment process, these instructions will help you prepare for your participant home visit.

Please ensure that you are currently logged in at Databrary to view protected content in this webpage. For confidentiality reasons, access to video exemplars is restricted to researchers with authorized access to Databrary. To register for access, visit http://databrary.org/register.


At the end of every home visit (for each participant), you will upload the following:

  1. exactly 5 videos (to Databrary repository)
      - One-hour natural play video + weather reading from phone
      - Video of infant hand measurement
      - House walkthrough (including measurements of each room, naming flooring, cleaning equipment and supplies, safety equipment, air purifiers, humidifiers, vents, pets)
      - Video of dust collection in each ofthe 2-3 areas (or from vacuum cleaner,if needed
      - Administration of parent-report questionnaires

  2. exactly 3 questionnaire files (through the KoBo Toolbox app)
      - demographics questionnaire from the phone call
      - DUST Home Questionnaire (health questionnaire, motor skills questionnaire, home environment and cleaning practices questionnaire)
      - post-visit notes

1. Arrival introduction

Note: Researchers should always act in a professional and respectful manner when interacting with the participants and while in the home. Do not make comments or react to anything in front of the mom to make her feel uncomfortable. All families should feel that their participation in the study is meaningful.

Say: Hi, my name is [NAME] and I’m visiting from [INSTITUTION]. Thanks for letting us come to your home today!

  • Take off your shoes before entering or just after entering and place them near the door.
  • Ask for a good place to hide all objects that you are bringing to the home to keep these objects out of the infant’s reach (backpack and any personal items such as coat, water bottle, etc.). Do not leave any equipment lying around.
  • Do not engage or warm up to the baby. Just make caregiver feel comfortable with your presence

2. Consent to participate (not on camera)

Experimenter should explain the study and ask mom to sign the consent.

Say: The visit has a few parts. I’ll begin by video-recording you and [CHILD] as you go about your day. I will video-record you both for one hour. Afterwards, I’ll ask you to give me a walk-through of your home that I will record on video to get a sense of the places [CHILD] goes and things that he/she plays with. I’ll also be measuring the dimensions of each room, video record your primary cleaning products and equipment, and vacuum three small areas of your home. I will measure [CHILD]’s hand size. Finally, I will ask you some general questions about your family and home, and about [CHILD]’s skills. Everything will be video-recorded and the visit will last for no more than three hours.You will receive $100 for your participation at the end. Do you have any questions? Okay, great. Here is the consent form that explains everything I just said. Please read it through and then print and sign your name on the back .

3. One-hour natural play video

  1. Say: “So I’m going to start recording now.”

2)Begin recording and give instructions. Say:“For the next hour, do anything you would typically do as if I weren’t here. Try to ignore me as much as possible and I will stay out of the way. I will also try not to respond to you and [CHILD] so that he/she is not distracted. You can go anywhere in your home but we just ask you to remain indoors. You can play together or not, you can do chores, watch TV, talk on the phone, give him/her a bath or snack. The idea is to capture what your typical day is like. And as a reminder, I’m just here to record so you will be in charge of [CHILD]’s safety.”

3)Record weather information (temperature in C° or F°, air quality, UV index, wind, precipitation, visibility, humidity, pressure) on smart phone (show in the beginning of natural play) Record weather information (temperature in C° or F°, air quality, UV index, wind, precipitation, visibility, humidity, pressure) on smart phone (show in the beginning of natural play).

4)Record for 1 hour (60 minutes of uninterrupted natural play recoding, in addition to the time you took you to give instructions and record the weather app).

General Recording Guidelines

General Recording Guidelines ● Aim to get 60 minutes of uninterrupted natural play, in addition to the time you take to give instructions. ● Keep camera on the child at all times. Specifically, ensure that the child’s whole body is visible on camera. If caregiver is in frame, capture as much of their body as possible without compromising the view of the child. ● Record while standing in front or to the side of the child as much as possible. ● Do not zoom in. ● Always try to stay toward the edge of rooms and doorways. You do not want to influence the child to interact with you or get in the child’s way. ● Remain as far a distance as possible (~3 to 5 m, hugging the wall) so that the child is not distracted by your presence. ● Do not interact with the child or make eye contact with the child. Just watch the child through the viewfinder of the camera. ● If caregiver asks you to pause the recording (e.g., to go to the toilet, breastfeed child, change child’s diaper), ask for permission to point the camera away so that you can continue recording audio. Or with caregiver’s permission, you can record the caregiver’s feet or another non-sensitive body part (during breastfeeding, or a diaper change).

Video capture tips:

  • Watch recording through viewfinder to ensure that the view is not blurry or shaky
  • Move the camera slowly and walk slowly
  • A clear and steady view, free of blurriness and shakiness, is necessary for detailed coding of the home environment
  • The laser measure device does not work against reflective surfaces (mirrors, glass walls, etc), so point to a non-reflective surface or measure from the reflective surface towards the opposite direction.

Room measurements:

  • You will measure all rooms in the house while you do the video walkthrough
  • A room is any space used by someone on a regular basis - bedrooms, kitchens, bathrooms, hallways, bathrooms, basements - rooms don’t need to have windows
  • A room has to have a clear demarcation (e.g., a wall or an entry). A space is considered to be a room if it has a minimum of 3 walls.
  • If the room has a short divider (e.g., when a kitchen and a living room are divided by a counter), count as one big room and measure accordingly.

Steps:

  1. Start at the entrance of the home.
  2. Pause at the entrance of each room.
  3. First, get as much of the Entire Room in frame as possible. Keep the camera zoomed out and make sure to capture the ceiling and the floor of the room..
  4. Audibly name the room by its function (e.g., This is where [CHILD] sleeps)..
  5. Next, pan the camera SLOWLY from Left to Right.
  6. Then, pan the camera to Floor, name all of the different types of surfaces on the floor in the space (hardwood, plush carpet, thin rug, linoleum, tile, foam mat,etc.), and then pan to the Ceiling.
  7. Ask caregiver if child has access and spends time in each room: Does [CHILD] have access and spend any time in this room?
  8. Ensure during the house walk-through that the caregiver provides information on all of the following: o Floor Cleaning Equipment.“Please show me your floor cleaning equipment like brooms/mops and vacuums (show brand or model).” Ask caregiver to take out or reveal cleaning equipment (mops, brooms, vacuums, etc.) from closets or cabinets to video record them. o Floor Cleaning Supplies and Products. “Please show me the floor cleaning products and supplies you use on a regular basis.” Ask caregiver to take out products from under sinks and closets to video record the labels. o Air circulation. Researcher should ask about exhaust fans (bathroom), range hood ( kitchen), humidifiers, and air purifiers. “Is there an exhaust/range hood/ humidifiers/air purifiers in this room.” o Child locks and any other safety measures. o Pets. If a pet (especially dogs or cats) are in the home, video record the pet for about 10s to get information about pet breed and hair/fur. o Record ventilation, exhaust fans, vents, humidifiers, etc.
  9. Hold the camera in one hand while you take measurements of the room using the other. o Turn laser measuring device on by pressing ON/DIST button. Make sure the laser beam is visible. o Measure wall to wall, lengthwise and widthwise. o If a room has an odd or asymmetrical shape (i.e., any shape other than a rectangle or a square), measure the largest rectangle or square area of the room. o Place the base of the laser device flat on the wall, push ON/DIST again to send the beam across the room (avoid moldings, door castings, reflective surfaces) o Repeat the above for the second dimension (length or width) o Focus camera on laser measure for each measure and read numbers out loud with units (e.g., “eight point five feet”) 10.Do NOT turn off the camera when walking to next room. 11.Walk SLOWLY. 12.When all rooms are recorded, walk back to the entrance of the homen and then stop recording.

Dust Collection

< Insert video>

● Collect dust samples from the living room and the room where the child sleeps (could be child’s own bedroom or a shared space with caregivers, etc.). In 1/3 collections, collect sample from primary entryway to home. ● Position the tripod so you can see the area of the floor you will be vacuuming and the equipment. ● Set down the metal template. Try to put it in the center of the room or on carpet if the room has a throw rug. ● Set down the placemat and put your supplies on the placemat. ● Set down your timing device next to the template. ● Start recording. ● Put on gloves, insert the sock into the neck of the vacuum, and secure the nozzle. ● Turn the vacuum on and position it over the template. ● Start your timer ● Vacuum for 5 minutes in an up/down columnar pattern from one side to the other side of the template ● Point vacuum upward and shut it off. It’s important to hold the vacuum upward before turning it off so no dust falls out. ● Remove the sock from the vacuum, secure the sock with the twist-tie, wrap in aluminum foil and place in a sandwich bag. ● Clean the inlet hose of vacuum. ● If the target room has no carpet, then vacuum 2 template-sized adjoining areas. ● If no rooms have carpet and you don’t fill the sock to the marked region, then ask caregiver if they will allow you to take dust from their home vacuum cleaner. Put the sock into our vacuum cleaner near the neck, hold the sock tightly by the neck, and then turn it on for 5s while the nozzle is inside their vacuum cannister or bag.

You will need to change the battery on the camera to ensure sufficient power. ● Administer the questionnaires on KBTB in the following order: o Milestones o Health o Typical day o Division of labor o Home environment/cleaning o Databrary form signing.
